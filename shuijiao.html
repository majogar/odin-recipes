Dumpling 水餃

A member of the large family of Chinese dumplings jiaozi, shuǐjiǎo — meaning simply boiled dumplings — is a dish which is both everyday and festive. Served as a street food, in traditional restaurants, and on special occasions
Now Lets start cooking

Ingredients

  • Wheat Flour
  • Soy Sauce
  • Pork
  • Ginger
  • Shaoxing wine
  • Sunflower Oil
  • Sugar
  • Salt

Preparation

Dough
  1. Mix wheat or rice flour, water, and a little salt.
  2. Start kneading and if it feels sticky, add some flour.
  3. After kneading, the dough should rest to develop gluten which will make it more elastic.
  4. Then, the dough is rolled into strips and cut into pieces which will each make an individual dumpling. Each piece should be rolled into a thin circle
Filling
  1. Meat is often combined with ginger, mushrooms, garlic chives, scallions, Asian shallots, cucumber, etc.
Shape the dumplings
  1. Adjust the amount of filling to the size of the dough circle — 1 to 1 ½ tbsp should be enough.
  2. To seal the dumplings, moisten the wrapper edges with some water and fold them in half, pleating the edges, into a crescent shape.
  3. Keep the dough and the assembled dumplings under a wet cloth to prevent them from drying.
The final stage

Dumplings are cooked in a large pot of boiling water which is sometimes slightly salted or oiled.
